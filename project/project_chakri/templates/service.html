A GLOBAL CO2 EMISSION ANALYSIS

In our data analytics project, "Unearthing The Environmental Impact Of Human Activity: A Global CO2 Emission," we obtained comprehensive data spanning the years 1947 to 2022 for all countries.
This extensive dataset provided us with a historical perspective on the evolution of CO2 emissions, allowing us to track changes over nearly eight decades.
By analyzing this wealth of data, we were able to uncover trends, identify key drivers, and assess the impact of various factors on global carbon output.
From the post-war industrialization era to the modern-day challenges of climate change, our dataset enabled us to capture the full spectrum of human activity's influence on the environment.
This robust historical data served as the foundation for our dashboards, stories, and visualizations, empowering stakeholders with actionable insights to address the pressing issue of climate change.
As we conclude our project, we recognize the significance of this comprehensive dataset in advancing our understanding of the environmental consequences of human activity and the urgent need for sustainable solutions.

DASHBOARD

Our project has meticulously crafted visualizations using Tableau Desktop to shed light on critical aspects of global CO2 emissions.
Through pie charts, we highlight the top 5 countries with the highest flaring CO2 emissions and gas CO2 emissions, providing a clear representation of the geographic distribution of these emissions sources.
These visualizations offer valuable insights into the major contributors to specific types of CO2 emissions, informing discussions on mitigation strategies and international cooperation.
Additionally, our line chart tracks the percentage of global CO2 emissions from 1947 to 2022, offering a comprehensive view of historical trends and milestones.
This visualization captures the evolution of CO2 emissions over time, from post-war industrialization to modern-day sustainability efforts, serving as a powerful tool for policymakers, researchers, and the public alike to understand the trajectory of human impact on the environment.

Evolution of Trade-Related CO2 Emissions: 1990-2020

The data provided showcases the evolution of trade-related CO2 emissions over several decades. Beginning in 1990, there's a notable increase in CO2 emissions attributable to trade, reflecting the growing interconnectedness of global economies. This rise continues until the mid-2000s, peaking in 2008, likely influenced by increased globalization and industrialization.
However, following the global financial crisis in 2008, there appears to be a temporary downturn in trade-related CO2 emissions, indicative of the economic slowdown and subsequent reduction in global trade activity. Yet, as economies recovered, emissions started climbing again, reaching significant levels by 2013.
From 2013 onwards, there seems to be a fluctuating pattern, perhaps reflecting changes in trade policies, economic growth rates, and environmental regulations across various regions. Nonetheless, the general trend suggests that trade-related CO2 emissions remain a significant contributor to global carbon emissions, underscoring the importance of sustainable trade practices and environmental policies in mitigating climate change.
